What is Quishing?

Quishing is a form of phishing that uses QR codes to trick users into visiting malicious websites or downloading harmful software. Attackers embed malicious links within a QR code, and when scanned, it can lead the user to a fraudulent website designed to steal personal information or distribute malware.

How Quishing Works

Quishing attacks typically involve a QR code that is placed in an email, physical location, or even a website. When the QR code is scanned by a victim, it redirects to a phishing website or downloads malicious content. Often, these codes are placed on legitimate materials like flyers or posters, making them seem trustworthy.

Common Examples

  • Fake promotional offers that lead to malicious websites.
  • QR codes on restaurant menus that redirect to phishing pages.
  • Fake delivery or shipment notifications containing QR codes.

How to Protect Yourself

To avoid falling victim to quishing, always be cautious when scanning QR codes from unknown or unexpected sources. Here are some best practices:

  • Verify the source of the QR code before scanning.
  • Use a QR code scanner that checks the link destination before visiting it.
  • Be wary of QR codes in unsolicited emails or on suspicious websites.
